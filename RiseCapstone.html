Feedback vs. Coaching cases

  • Before I started Rise one my manager wants me to focus on "give critical feedback" and "leading discussions" skills. Giving critical feedback is challenging to me. But I found giving Critical feedback is easier using "One Minute Redirect" technique:
    Give Redirect as soon as possible. When I meet with one of my direct report I confirm the facts, review the mistake together (be specific). Express how I feel about the mistake's impact on result. Then be quite on a moment to allow the person to feel concerned about it. Then tell them they are better than mistake, I think well of them in person, I still have confidence in them. I have used this technique. Critical feedback with Redirect helps developing my team and keeping our project on track.
  • After Rise session I had a opportunity to mentor two Data Scientists outside our group. Both of them are in Consulting org and doing different kind of work. We have monthly recurring meetings. I showed them the work CI group is doing and encourage them to find a suitable ML project using any existing dataset the have access to. They both found a interesting projects to explore. I frequently used coaching in our monthly meetings. Besides sharing useful resources and knowledge, I asked a lot of thought-provoking questions to get them motivated.

Virtual Teams, Collaboration Skills cases

  • During Rise session two I learned "fear of conflict" is one of five "Dysfunctions of a Team" book. Communicating over Microsoft Team is a challenge. We used to walk over the person's desk to talk about issues and draw on the whiteboard. I challenged myself to ask questions in many of our group's weekly virtual syncs and share knowledge, experience. Our team's collaboration increased this year even comparing to last year when we were meeting face-to-face. We learned many things from each other. We also found sharing code on Github is much better. Our team's use of Github went up. At this point our team prefer to conduct our three weekly syncs over Team than face-to-face.
  • Encourage practice "Shut-down Ritual" to help reduce burnout. I encourage my teammates to use "shut-down ritual" every night and organize team virtual event. This is harder to measure but necessary for the long term success and retentions.
  • Adjusted 1-on-1 meeting to be shorter, each team member share a 1-on-1 OneNote with me. All my direct reports starts to do that. The note will document problem solved and outstanding issues. The note is also a place to organize knowledge and tasks at hand. I also have better understanding any problems they have. I can be more proactive to address any problems.
